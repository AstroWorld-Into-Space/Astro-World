I have two research work on solid state physics which are in the process of publication.

Effect of Deposition-Time On Chemical Bath Deposited Manganese-Doped Zinc Selenide Films.

Manganese doped zinc selenide (ZnSe:Mn) films were deposited on glass substrates using the simple chemical bath deposition (CBD) method in an alkaline medium at a bath temperature of 90 degree Celcius and deposition time was varied from 30 to 90 minutes. The films were characterized to obtain the optical properties and structural properties, microstructural properties and elemental properties using the Cary 300 UV/Visible Spectrophotometer- at normal incidence of light in the wavelength range of 200nm-800nm and the Rigaku miniflex 630 diffractometer, respectively and results from the work shows that the crystalline growth in the films were reduced as deposition time increased.

Effect of Molarity Concentration On Chemical Bath Deposited Manganese-Doped Zinc Selenide Films.

This is a co-joint work. Here Manganese doped zinc selenide (ZnSe:Mn) films were deposited on glass substrates using the simple chemical bath deposition (CBD) method in an alkaline medium at a bath temperature of 90 degree Celcius and molarity concentration was varied from 0.05 mol to 0.1 mol. The films were characterized to obtain the optical properties and structural properties, microstructural properties and elemental properties using the Cary 300 UV/Visible Spectrophotometer- at normal incidence of light in the wavelength range of 200nm-800nm and the Rigaku miniflex 630 diffractometer, respectively.
